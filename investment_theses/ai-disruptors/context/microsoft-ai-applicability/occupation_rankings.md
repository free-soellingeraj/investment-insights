# AI Applicability by Occupation

## Highest Applicability (Score 0.30+)
| Occupation Group | Score | Key Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Media & Communication Workers | 0.38 | Content creation, editing, commercial writing |
| Sales Representatives, Services | 0.35 | Customer communication, information provision |
| Information & Record Clerks | 0.33 | Data processing, record maintenance |
| Mathematical Science Occupations | 0.32 | Data analysis, computational tasks |
| Postsecondary Teachers | 0.31 | Knowledge synthesis, material preparation |

## Lowest Applicability (Score ≤0.07)
| Occupation Group | Score | Why Low |
|---|---|---|
| Firefighting & Prevention Workers | 0.07 | Physical presence required |
| Protective Service Supervisors | 0.07 | Physical/situational judgment |
| Construction Trades Workers | 0.07 | Manual/equipment operation |
| Material Moving Workers | 0.07 | Physical labor |
| Forest & Conservation Workers | 0.03 | Lowest — outdoor physical work |

## Dominant IWA Clusters (What AI Is Actually Used For)
All cluster around **"information work"** — the creation, processing, and communication of information:

- **Creation:** Edit written materials, write commercial material, generate content
- **Processing:** Maintain knowledge, analyze data, synthesize information
- **Communication:** Respond to customer inquiries, provide information, explain concepts

## Critical Insight
**Most occupations have at least some AI applicability** — because information processing
exists in nearly all roles. The range is 0.03 to 0.38, not 0.00 to 1.00. This means
disruption potential is not binary but a spectrum.

## Demographic & Economic Notes
- Weak wage correlation (r=0.13): AI applicability is NOT concentrated in high-wage jobs
- Broad across education levels: no strong education-based pattern
- Potential inequality reduction: AI may democratize expertise and narrow skill gaps
