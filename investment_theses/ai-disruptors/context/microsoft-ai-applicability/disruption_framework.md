# Disruption vs Augmentation Framework

## Two Patterns of AI Integration

### Pattern 1: Task Delegation (High AI-Action Applicability)
- **Who:** Financial operations, business management roles
- **Behavior:** Workers hand off entire activities to AI, shift focus to remaining human-essential tasks
- **Example IWAs delegated:** Communication drafting, training material creation
- **Implication:** Job restructuring — the role changes shape as delegated tasks disappear from human workflow
- **Disruption signal:** Companies automating these delegated activities at scale

### Pattern 2: Workflow Collaboration (High User-Goal Applicability)
- **Who:** Computer/mathematical professionals, analysts
- **Behavior:** Workers use AI to enhance performance within their existing activities
- **Example:** Data scientists using AI for analytical assistance (AI augments, doesn't replace)
- **Implication:** Productivity amplification — same tasks, done faster/better
- **Disruption signal:** Companies building AI copilots/assistants for specific professional workflows

## Conversation-Level Asymmetry
- 40% of conversations have **completely disjoint** IWA sets (user vs AI side)
- 96% contain more IWAs unique to one side than in common
- AI performs foundational work (research, synthesis, formatting) that **supports but differs from** what users request
- This means AI creates value by doing adjacent/supporting work, not just the requested task

## Why Applicability ≠ Job Replacement
The paper explicitly warns against equating high scores with job loss:

1. **ATM Paradox:** ATMs automated teller transactions but caused branch proliferation, ultimately *increasing* teller employment (workers shifted to relationship-building)
2. **New occupation emergence:** Majority of current employment is in occupations that arose in the last 100 years due to new technologies
3. **Connecting glue:** Decomposing occupations into tasks misses the integration work that holds tasks together — this is often irreducibly human

## What Applicability *Does* Predict
- Where AI tools will find product-market fit
- Which workflows will be restructured (not eliminated)
- Where productivity gains are achievable
- Where new AI-native roles/companies can emerge
